Most consultancies sell you a thing they read about. We got tired of that, so we did something slightly reckless: we handed our own company to the machines first, and only then started selling the idea to anyone else.

## What "run by AI" actually means here

It's easy to say "AI-powered" and mean "we used ChatGPT to write the newsletter". That's not this. Automancer's finance operations — invoicing, reconciliation, the bookkeeping grind — run on agents. Project tracking runs on agents: every lane governed, monitored, and board-gated before anything touches a client. This website was written and built by agents. The field note you're reading was drafted by one.

None of that is a demo we spun up for the pitch. It's how the lights stay on.

## The line we won't cross

Here's the part that matters, and it's the reason a care provider will hand us the spine of their business: **a human makes every decision and takes every call.** The agents draft, calculate, monitor and propose. Waseem decides. Every invoice that goes out, every recommendation that reaches a client, every judgement call — a person made it, on purpose.

That's not a compromise we're apologising for. It's the whole design. The agents are the hands. The head stays human.

## Why we're this open about it

Two reasons. One is honesty: you deserve to know what's automated and what isn't, and pretending a person answered when an agent did is exactly the kind of thing we hate about the rest of the industry.

The other is that it *is* the pitch. If we can run an entire consultancy this way — lean, fast, cheap enough to publish our prices — then taking the weight off your business isn't a leap of faith. We already ran the experiment. On ourselves. It's not magic. It's very good engineering.
